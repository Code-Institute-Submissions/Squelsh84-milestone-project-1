About Us

Michael learned how to cut his first stick from his late father, and other stick makers.The sticks he makes are not made for sale but are only for gifts to fellow walkers. Hazel , Ash and Blacthorn from Tipperary, Kilkenny, Laois, and Connemara is normally used. The stick is first cut in the Winter, between November and March and left in a cool dry place for about 12 months. It is then steamed to straighten it, cleaned, sanded and shaped to suit the end user. A ferrule (iron ring) is then added to the base, a strap to the handle and finally, the stick is treated with boiled linseed oil or Danish oil.
